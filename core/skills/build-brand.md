# Skill: Build Brand Strategy (100M Branding Framework)

## Purpose

> **Output posture:** Present observations with questions, not conclusions. Show calculations. Invite disagreement. See `core/methodology/DISCOVERY_NOT_PRONOUNCEMENT.md`.

Deliberately builds, grows, or pivots a brand for maximum profit by engineering strategic brand associations that lower customer acquisition cost, increase lifetime value, and build lasting competitive advantage.

**The Core Principle:**
> Branding means pairing your business with things your ideal customers already like, trust, or aspire to.

## Trigger
Use this skill when:
- Customer wants to build or strengthen their brand
- Analyzing why a brand isn't converting
- Pivoting to a new market or audience
- Evaluating brand associations and positioning
- Customer is "invisible" in their market
- Competing on price instead of brand value

## Key Definitions

### What Branding IS:
```
BRANDING = Pairing your business with things your ideal customers like

Good Branding → Pair with things majority of ideal audience LIKES
Bad Branding  → Pair with things majority of ideal audience DISLIKES
No Branding   → You're invisible - exposure results in no behavior change
```

### What Branding is NOT:
- Logos, colors, fonts (those are design)
- Feelings, intuition, vibes (too vague to act on)
- Something that "just happens" (it must be deliberate)

## Measuring Brand

### Three Variables:

| Variable | Definition | Measurement |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| **Reach** | How many people know about you | Audience size, impressions |
| **Influence** | Likelihood your brand changes behavior | Engagement, response rates |
| **Direction** | Which way behavior changes | Towards (buy) or Away (avoid) |

### Brand Health Formula:
```
Strong Brand = High Reach + High Influence + Towards Direction

Weak Brand = Low Reach OR Low Influence OR Away Direction
```

### The Ultimate Measure: Money
```
Good branding = Audience likes AND buys
Bad branding = Audience dislikes AND doesn't buy

Did you make more money? That's the answer.
```

## The 4-Step Branding Process

### Step 1: Figure Out Your Ideal Customer

**The 4 Criteria:**

| Criteria | Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Growing** | Is this market expanding? | Growing market = automatic growth |
| **Has Money** | Can they afford premium prices? | No money = no premium pricing |
| **Easy to Target** | Can you reach them with ads/content? | Can't find = can't sell |
| **In Pain** | Do they desperately want what you sell? | No pain = no urgency to buy |

**Avatar Questions:**
1. Who specifically do you want to serve?
2. Is that market growing or shrinking?
3. Do they have money to spend?
4. Where do they congregate (easy targeting)?
5. What painful problem do they have?

---

### Step 2: Figure Out What They Like

**Categories to explore:**

- **Outcomes they want** (more money, more time, more status)
- **Problems they hate** (pain points, frustrations, fears)
- **People they admire** (influencers, leaders, celebrities in their world)
- **Content they consume** (podcasts, books, channels, publications)
- **Values they hold** (beliefs, principles, what they stand for)
- **Experiences they enjoy** (events, activities, lifestyle elements)
- **Products they already buy** (adjacent purchases, luxury items)

**Discovery Questions:**
1. What do they want MORE of?
2. What do they want LESS of?
3. Who do they follow and admire?
4. What content do they binge?
5. What values do they publicly claim?
6. What would they brag about?

---

### Step 3: Associate Your Brand With Things They Like

**The Bouquet Metaphor:**
```
Individual flowers ≠ bouquet
Individual associations ≠ brand

Tying many flowers together = bouquet
Tying many associations together = brand
```

**Association Channels:**

| Channel | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Content** | Create valuable content they consume | Videos, articles, podcasts, PDFs |
| **People** | Appear next to people they admire | Collaborations, interviews, endorsements |
| **Experiences** | Create experiences they enjoy | Events, communities, moments |
| **Products** | Make products that deliver outcomes | Free and paid offerings |
| **Values** | Embody values they identify with | Messaging, actions, stands you take |

**Levels of Authority (Weakest to Strongest):**
```
1. What YOU Say       → Ads, content, outreach (least influential)
2. What OTHERS Say    → Affiliates, testimonials, referrals
3. What THEY Experience → Using your product (most influential)
```

---

### Step 4: Optimize Associations for Maximum Profit

**5 Directions to Expand:**

| Direction | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Up Market** | Larger/wealthier customers | Solo → Multi-location owners |
| **Down Market** | Smaller/emerging customers | Enterprises → Startups |
| **Adjacent** | Similar but different market | Gyms → Yoga studios |
| **Broader** | Entire category | Dog trainers → Pet businesses |
| **Narrower** | Slice of existing market | All coaches → Executive coaches only |

**The Bouquet Adjustment:**
- **Narrower** = Only red roses (specific associations)
- **Broader** = All types of flowers (diverse associations)
- **Adjacent** = Swap red roses for yellow roses (shift associations)
- **Up/Down** = Change flower quality (premium vs accessible)

## Brand Pivots: Net Change Analysis

**Every pivot has two outcomes:**

```
GOOD TRADE: Gain more audience than you lose
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Lost: ●●●                              │
│  Gained: ●●●●●●●●●●●●●●                 │
│  NET: +11 (Worth it)                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘

BAD TRADE: Lose more audience than you gain
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Lost: ●●●●●●●●●●●●●●                   │
│  Gained: ●●●                            │
│  NET: -11 (Not worth it)                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Pivot Evaluation Questions:**
1. Who will we LOSE with this change?
2. Who will we GAIN with this change?
3. Is the net positive or negative?
4. Does the new audience buy what we sell?

---

## Dealing with Brand Mistakes

**When bad associations happen:**

```
Don't try to ELIMINATE the bad association.
It happened. You can't undo it.

Instead: OVERWHELM with good associations.
```

**Recovery Strategy:**
1. Acknowledge (if necessary)
2. Don't dwell or keep apologizing
3. Immediately flood with positive associations
4. Keep making and keeping promises
5. Let time dilute the negative

**The Kanye Principle:** Even after major controversy, continued great products and positive associations can minimize bad ones over time.

## Example Analysis (Abbreviated)

**Input:**
> "I'm a business coach for women entrepreneurs but I'm not standing out. Everyone seems the same in my market."

**Output:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    BRAND STRATEGY ANALYSIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BRAND: Women's Business Coach
PROBLEM: Invisible in crowded market

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                  STEP 1: IDEAL CUSTOMER
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TARGET AVATAR: "Women entrepreneurs, $100K-$500K revenue,
               service-based, want to scale to $1M+"

CRITERIA CHECK:
☑ Growing: Yes - women entrepreneurship rising
☑ Has Money: Yes - already making $100K+
☑ Easy to Target: Yes - Facebook groups, podcasts, events
☑ In Pain: Yes - stuck at revenue ceiling

AVATAR SCORE: 4/4 ✓

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                  STEP 2: WHAT THEY LIKE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OUTCOMES: Scale without burnout, predictable revenue, team
PROBLEMS: Wearing all hats, feast/famine, imposter syndrome
PEOPLE: Sara Blakely, Marie Forleo, specific industry leaders
VALUES: Authenticity, balance, impact, legacy
CONTENT: Podcasts, Instagram, live events

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                  DIAGNOSIS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PROBLEM: Generic associations - "empowerment," "mindset,"
"abundance" - same as every other coach

SOLUTION: Narrower positioning + specific outcome associations

RECOMMENDED: Go NARROWER
• Instead of "women entrepreneurs"
• Try: "Women service providers scaling from $250K to $1M
       who want to stop trading time for money"

NEW ASSOCIATIONS TO CREATE:
• Content: "How I helped [client] go from $300K to $1.2M
           in 18 months without hiring a team"
• People: Collaborate with SaaS founders, systems experts
• Values: "Profit over hustle" - contrarian to "grind" culture
```

**Common Belief Blocks to Strong Branding:**

| Weak Branding Behavior | Underlying Belief |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Won't charge premium prices | "I'm not worth it" |
| Associates with everyone | "I can't afford to be selective" |
| Avoids strong positioning | "I'll alienate people" |
| Copies competitors | "They know something I don't" |
| Changes messaging constantly | "Nothing I do works" |
| Won't appear with bigger names | "I don't belong there" |

## The Branding Cycle

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                             │
│   1. WHAT YOU SAY                                           │
│      (Advertising, Content, Outreach)                       │
│                    │                                        │
│                    ▼                                        │
│   2. FIRST PURCHASE                                         │
│      (Based on brand associations)                          │
│                    │                                        │
│                    ▼                                        │
│   3. PRODUCT EXPERIENCE                                     │
│      (Delivers on promises OR doesn't)                      │
│                    │                                        │
│          ┌────────┴────────┐                                │
│          ▼                 ▼                                │
│   GOOD EXPERIENCE    BAD EXPERIENCE                         │
│          │                 │                                │
│          ▼                 ▼                                │
│   4. WHAT OTHERS SAY  4. WHAT OTHERS SAY                    │
│      (Positive WOM)      (Negative WOM)                     │
│          │                 │                                │
│          ▼                 ▼                                │
│   VIRTUOUS CYCLE     VICIOUS CYCLE                          │
│   (Brand grows)      (Brand dies)                           │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key Insight:** Your advertising builds brand short-term. Your product builds brand long-term.

## One-Page Branding Checklist

From the original playbook:

- [ ] Figure out ideal avatar (Growing, Pain, Money, Easy to Find)
- [ ] Figure out what they like
- [ ] Associate with stuff they like through:
  - [ ] Content they like
  - [ ] Products they like (free and paid)
  - [ ] People they like
- [ ] Grow strategically (Up/Down/Adjacent/Broader/Narrower)
- [ ] More advertising = more reach
- [ ] Products match promises (virtuous cycle)
- [ ] When mistakes happen, overwhelm with good
- [ ] Wait 5 years and don't give up

## Notes

- Branding is DELIBERATE - most brands happen by accident
- Good branding = lower CAC + higher LTV + lower risk
- Brand goes BOTH ways - product affects brand, brand affects product
- Patience is essential - 5+ year time horizon
- Trace the belief when the customer resists strong positioning — it's rarely about the market, usually about identity
- Use `/validate-idea` before major brand pivots
