## Rules

- Every pattern is **signed by a practitioner** (not by "the agency" or "the team"). Attribution stays on the pattern even as it evolves.
- Every signature carries a **lens** — the cognitive angle the pattern was observed through. Different profiles see different patterns, and a pattern named by one lens is provisional until a second lens confirms it. See `core/methodology/PERSON_PROFILE_STANDARD.md` § "Profile Fit (Operational)."
- A pattern is not a tip. A pattern is: *when X conditions are present, Y is usually true, because Z.* If you cannot state the conditions and the mechanism, it is not a pattern yet.
- Patterns with evidence from **at least two clients** are candidates for the cross-agency `KNOWN_PATTERNS.md`. One-client patterns stay local until confirmed.
- Reviewing each other's patterns is a habit, not a favour. Peer review is how a claim becomes a pattern. Peer review by a *different profile lens* is stronger than same-lens review.
