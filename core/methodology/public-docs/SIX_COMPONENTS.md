# The Six Components of an Agency Operating System

An agency's operating system has six parts. Each has a name.

The seven habits describe *how a practitioner works.* The six components describe *what the operating system is made of.* Two complementary lenses on the same operating system.

---

## Ladder

**Where the agency stands on its AI adoption journey, honestly named.**

The AI Adoption Ladder runs from L0 (occasional ChatGPT user) to L9 (live full-stack diagnostic engine on real client data). Every practitioner is at some level. Knowing which level — without shame, without pretense — is the starting point for every other decision. A practitioner at L1 is not behind one at L8. They are earlier in the same work.

## Layers

**Where in the client's business the problem actually lives.**

The 7+1 Layer model — L0 Source, L1 Core, L2 Identity, L3 Product, L4 Offer, L5 Channels, L6 Customer, L7 Market — is the diagnostic map. Constraints show up as symptoms at L5 (channels) but often live at L2 (identity) or L1 (core). The agency that can name which layer the constraint actually sits at is the agency that stops being interchangeable.

## Trail

**A source you can point to for every claim.**

Every claim in every deliverable carries an evidence tag — `[DATA]`, `[OBSERVED]`, `[STATED]`, `[NARRATIVE]`, `[INFERRED]`, `[HEARSAY]`. A recommendation without a trail is a guess. AI generates plausible-sounding numbers by default; the trail is the only defense against confident-wrong outputs being treated as data.

## Rhythms

**Loops around projects, reviews, retros.**

Delivery is continuous, not one-shot. Projects have start and end dates, but learning does not. Every project closes with a post-mortem and at least one signed pattern. Weekly reviews track the scorecard. Quarterly sessions revisit Identity, Offer, ICP. Annual reflection revisits the founder's belief field. Each rhythm catches habit drift at its layer's natural cadence.

## Memory

**What the system remembers so people don't have to.**

The operating system writes things down — client knowledge, evidence trails, patterns, decisions, retros — in a form that the next human (and the next AI) can read. Lessons that live only in senior people's heads leave when those people leave. Lessons that live in the system compound. Memory is the property that makes an agency survive its founders and the tools they started with.

## Accord

**Humans and AI sharpening each other on real work.**

The agency's thinking is legible to the machine. The machine's output is legible to the humans. Both get sharper every time either touches real work. Accord is not automation — automation replaces the human. Accord assumes the human is irreplaceable and makes the machine useful to them.

---

## Why six, not five or seven

These six are orthogonal — no component substitutes for another:

- Without **Ladder**, practitioners mistake each other's progress and over- or under-estimate what a client can do.
- Without **Layers**, diagnoses hit the wrong part of the business.
- Without **Trail**, the work cannot be defended or audited.
- Without **Rhythms**, habits drift silently.
- Without **Memory**, the agency can't compound across clients.
- Without **Accord**, the agency either ignores AI or surrenders to it — both fail.

Remove any one component and the operating system stops operating. Add a seventh and you are probably describing something that belongs inside one of the existing six.
