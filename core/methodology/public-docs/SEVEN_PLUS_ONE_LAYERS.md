# The 7+1 Layers

A framework for naming where in a client's business a problem actually lives. Eight layers, each with a natural half-life. Symptoms surface at the bottom layers (L5-L7) but constraints often live at the top (L0-L4). An agency that optimises L5 while the real constraint sits at L2 is optimising the wrong thing.

---

## L0 — Source

**Founder identity, belief field, the unsaid assumptions that shape everything else.**

The layer most agencies cannot touch because they cannot see it. What the founder believes about the business — about who the customer is, what the work is for, what money means, what "winning" looks like — sets the force field every other layer operates in. Marketing systems don't fail because of technology or tactics. They fail because the founder's belief system creates a field the system cannot function against.

**Signal:** founder says the same sentence about the company across every meeting, year after year, no matter what data arrives. The sentence is an L0 belief.
**Rhythm:** years. Shifts only on genuine reflection or crisis.

## L1 — Core

**Why the company exists. The reason the business is in business.**

Not the mission statement on the website. The actual answer to *"what would change if this company stopped existing tomorrow?"* An L1 constraint shows up as drift — the company is profitable but nobody remembers why they started, and new hires cannot articulate what the company is for.

**Signal:** staff, customers, and owner each give a different answer to *"what does this company do and why."*
**Rhythm:** years. Revisited annually; rarely changes more than once in a decade.

## L2 — Identity

**How the company shows up. What kind of company it is, not what it does.**

The identity layer is where the company's character lives — how it presents to clients, how it talks about itself, what it does and does not take on. Identity drift is the gap between the self the company *thinks it has* and the self the market *experiences.*

**Signal:** new clients arriving say things like *"I thought you were more of a [X] kind of agency"* — meaning the identity projected and the identity received don't match.
**Rhythm:** seasons. Revisited quarterly; shifts once a year or so.

## L3 — Product

**What the company actually delivers. The concrete work that leaves the building.**

Not the offer stack (that's L4) — the literal product. Decks, campaigns, code, reports, services. L3 drift is when the team is shipping something different from what the offer claims, or when the product has aged out of what the market wants.

**Signal:** delivery team says *"we keep doing this thing that nobody seems to want anymore,"* or the product has been the same shape for five years while the market has moved.
**Rhythm:** months. Reviewed monthly.

## L4 — Offer

**The stack, the packaging, the pricing. How the product is offered to the market.**

L4 sits between product (what you deliver) and channels (where you show up). An offer constraint shows up as *"we can get that cheaper"* pushback — when the offer is structured such that the market compares on price instead of on the offer's actual value.

**Signal:** the offer is being commoditised; price is the only axis of comparison.
**Rhythm:** seasons. Revisited quarterly.

## L5 — Channels

**Where the company shows up. Meta, Google, SEO, LinkedIn, email, events, PR.**

The layer agencies are most comfortable at. Most agency work happens here. L5 constraints are real — wrong channel for the audience, wrong creative for the channel, wrong attribution — but they are *downstream* of L0-L4. Fixing L5 while L2 is broken is optimising the visible while the invisible cracks widen.

**Signal:** campaigns perform inconsistently across channels with no clear pattern; channel performance does not correlate with revenue in any stable way.
**Rhythm:** weeks. Reviewed weekly.

## L6 — Customer

**Who the company serves. The customer the company is actually attracting vs. the one it says it targets.**

ICP drift is the classic L6 constraint. The company says "we serve X" but 60% of new deals look like Y. L6 also includes *who the customer becomes by buying from you* — the identity the customer gets by being your client. This matters because customer identity is a branding asset the company builds over time.

**Signal:** the ICP doc says one thing; the client roster says another; new deals tilt in a third direction.
**Rhythm:** seasons. Revisited quarterly.

## L7 — Market

**Macro forces the company cannot control. Regulation, currency, new entrants, supply chain, consumer-behaviour shifts, platform-level changes (Meta's policy changes, Google's SERP, Apple's ATT).**

L7 is where the company reads the weather. Not a layer the company optimises — a layer the company *watches* and adapts to. A company that ignores L7 gets surprised by regulatory changes or platform shifts that invalidate half its L5 work overnight.

**Signal:** a platform-level change affects the business in ways nobody saw coming; the company was optimising L5 while L7 shifted.
**Rhythm:** days to continuous. Weekly catch-up; acted on when a signal crosses a threshold.

## How the layers cascade

A constraint at a top layer (L0-L2) cascades downward. Belief shapes identity shapes product shapes offer shapes channels shapes customer shapes market response.

An agency diagnosing from the bottom up (starting at L5 where the metrics live) will often hit a false floor — the symptoms are at L5 but the fix lives three layers up. This is why the diagnostic skill is to *name the layer* before proposing the work. An L5 recommendation for an L2 problem is a wasted cycle for both sides.

## Why 7+1, not 7 or 8

L1-L7 are the seven operational layers — things the agency can work on and see the work of. L0 is the +1 — the source layer the agency cannot directly work on but whose influence shapes everything below. Most diagnostic work lives in L1-L7. The hardest work — the work with the biggest leverage — lives at L0.

## Natural rhythm per layer (summary)

| Layer | Rhythm | Revisited |
|---|---|---|
| L0 Source | Years | Annual founder reflection |
| L1 Core | Years | Annual |
| L2 Identity | Seasons | Quarterly |
| L3 Product | Months | Monthly |
| L4 Offer | Seasons | Quarterly |
| L5 Channels | Weeks | Weekly |
| L6 Customer | Seasons | Quarterly |
| L7 Market | Days to continuous | Continuous + weekly catch-up |

Rituals that ignore a layer's natural rhythm either over-impose structure or let the layer rot. See `../RITUALS_BY_LAYER.md` for the four-cadence spec that covers all eight layers.
