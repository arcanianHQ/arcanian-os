# The 7 Habits of a Successful Agency in the LLM / AI Era

The operating system has six parts. The practitioner who runs it has seven habits. These are the seven — the behaviours a practitioner enacts every day, at every layer of agency work.

---

## 1. Diagnose before you deliver

See the constraint above your work before you propose the work. Every brief starts with a diagnosis, not a deck. Present the finding clearly. Show the evidence. Ask the client where it doesn't match what they see on the ground. Inquiry is how professionals work together. Apologetic tentativeness is weakness wearing humility as a costume.

## 2. Show the trail

Every claim has a source you can point to. When you don't have one, you say *"I don't know yet, but I'll find out."* AI hands you precise-looking numbers — percentages, benchmarks, charts with trend lines — that turn out to be invented. Confident wrong is its default output. The trail is the only defense.

## 3. Close every loop

Projects are real — start dates, end dates, invoices. What cannot end with the project is the learning. Close with a post-mortem: what worked, what broke, what the next project starts from. A project that ends with an invoice extracted value. A project that ends with a loop built it — for both sides.

## 4. Work in accord with the machine

Use AI as a teammate whose output you can inspect. Keep your thinking legible to it. Keep its output legible to you. Neither replaces the other. Automation replaces the human. Accord assumes the human is irreplaceable and makes the machine useful to them — and to the next human who walks into the room cold.

## 5. Carry lessons through people

Agencies do not learn. People do. Patterns travel in conversation, in review, in a practitioner who sees the same shape a year later in a different business. And they travel through each practitioner differently — a pattern named by one lens is provisional until a second lens confirms it. Know whose lens you are reading. Systems help, but they serve the connection between people — they do not replace it.

## 6. Count on the people in the room

Strategy that counts *on* people holds. Strategy that counts *around* them collapses. The client, their partners, their franchisees, the ops manager who will live with what you recommend — they sit in the room, not across the table. They carry context you will never have. Ask their questions. Write their names into the plan. And know who they are — cognitive shape is as real as a role title, and a seat that fits the person holds while one that does not drifts. *Együtt* — together — is a working relationship, not a slogan.

## 7. Sharpen the instrument

The agency is the product. Invest in what compounds — craft, brand, memory, accord. Retire what does not — the billable hour, the tool-of-the-month, the deck nobody remembers. The renewal is the work.

---

## How the habits relate to the rest of the operating system

The habits are **continuous behaviours** that run at every layer. They are kept honest by **rituals** (weekly / monthly / quarterly / annual) at each layer's natural rhythm. They produce **artifacts** — documents that get filled in during rituals and refined by habits.

- **Habits** describe *how you work.* Principles, continuous.
- **Rituals** describe *when you check the work is still honest.* Scheduled pauses.
- **Artifacts** describe *what the work leaves behind.* Written evidence.

A habit you do not check at its layer's natural rhythm is a habit rotting in place. See `../RITUALS_BY_LAYER.md` for the cadence spec.
