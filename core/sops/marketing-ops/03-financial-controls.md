# 03 — Financial Controls

> Generic SOP. Adapt per client in `clients/{slug}/processes/`.
> Owner: Fractional CMO | Tier: 1 | Review: Monthly

## Purpose
Prevent uncontrolled marketing spend by enforcing budget cycles, approval thresholds, payment normalization, and vendor payment tracking.

## Trigger
- Start of monthly budget cycle
- New spend request exceeding threshold
- Invoice received from vendor or agency
- Debt or overdue payment detected
- Quarterly budget review

## Stakeholders (RACI)

| Role | R/A/C/I |
|---|---|
| Fractional CMO | A |
| Marketing Director | R (budget tracking, spend requests) |
| Finance Controller | R (payment processing, debt tracking) |
| Agency Account Manager(s) | C (invoice submission) |
| CEO / CMO (internal) | A (above-threshold approvals) |

## Systems
- Budget tracking spreadsheet or tool — monthly plan vs. actual, rolling forecast
- Accounting / ERP system — invoice processing, payment records
- Ad platform billing — direct debit or prepaid controls
- Shared drive — approved budgets, signed contracts, invoice archive

## Steps

### Monthly Budget Cycle
1. By last business day of prior month: Fractional CMO submits next month's marketing budget to Finance Controller and internal leadership for approval
2. Budget includes: channel-level allocation, agency fees, tool subscriptions, content production, contingency (5-10%)
3. Approved budget becomes the spend ceiling — no overspend without written approval
4. Marketing Director tracks actual spend weekly against approved budget in shared tracker
5. By 3rd business day of each month: Marketing Director delivers prior month's budget vs. actual report

### Spend Approval Thresholds
1. Define three tiers (adapt amounts per client):
   - **Tier A** (e.g., < 500 EUR): Marketing Director approves
   - **Tier B** (e.g., 500-5,000 EUR): Fractional CMO approves
   - **Tier C** (e.g., > 5,000 EUR): CEO / CMO (internal) approves
2. All approvals documented in writing (email or PM tool) before commitment
3. Emergency spend (unplanned, time-sensitive): Fractional CMO can approve up to Tier B, with retroactive documentation within 24h

### Payment Normalization
1. All recurring marketing payments (ad platforms, tools, agencies) must bill to a single company billing entity within 60 days of engagement start
2. No personal credit cards for ongoing marketing spend — transition to company billing during onboarding
3. Marketing Director maintains a payment method inventory: what bills where, card expiry dates, auto-renewal dates
4. 30 days before any card expiry or contract renewal: Marketing Director flags for review

### Debt Management
1. Finance Controller flags any overdue marketing invoices weekly
2. Overdue > 15 days: Marketing Director contacts vendor to confirm payment timeline
3. Overdue > 30 days: Fractional CMO escalates to internal leadership with impact assessment
4. No new spend committed to a vendor with outstanding debt > 30 days without CEO / CMO approval

### Vendor Payment Tracking
1. All vendor invoices logged in a shared tracker: vendor name, invoice date, amount, due date, payment status
2. Marketing Director reconciles vendor tracker against accounting records monthly
3. Discrepancies investigated and resolved within 5 business days
4. Quarterly: Fractional CMO reviews total vendor spend by category — flag vendors with >10% cost increase for renegotiation

## Escalation
- If actual spend exceeds approved budget by >5% in any month → Fractional CMO notifies CEO / CMO within 24h with explanation and corrective action
- If surprise invoice (not in approved budget) arrives → Marketing Director flags immediately, Fractional CMO decides: approve, dispute, or defer
- If vendor threatens service disruption due to payment → Finance Controller escalates same day

## KPIs

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Spend vs. approved plan variance | < 5% |
| Payments on company billing | 100% within 60 days |
| Surprise invoices (unbudgeted) | Zero |
| Monthly budget report delivery | By 3rd business day |

## Review Cadence
Monthly — Fractional CMO reviews spend vs. plan, payment normalization progress, and debt status. Quarterly deep review with Finance Controller and internal leadership.
