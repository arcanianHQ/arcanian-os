# 01 — Agency Coordination

> Generic SOP. Adapt per client in `clients/{slug}/processes/`.
> Owner: Fractional CMO | Tier: 1 | Review: Monthly

## Purpose
Align external agencies (PPC, SEO, creative, dev) on shared goals, prevent scope conflicts, and maintain a single source of truth for all marketing activity.

## Trigger
- New agency onboarded
- Weekly coordination cadence
- Agency offboarding initiated
- Scope conflict detected

## Stakeholders (RACI)

| Role | R/A/C/I |
|---|---|
| Fractional CMO | A |
| Marketing Director | R |
| Agency Account Manager(s) | R |
| Finance / Procurement | C |
| CEO / CMO (internal) | I |

## Systems
- Project management tool — shared task tracking, meeting notes
- Shared drive — briefs, reports, naming conventions doc
- Communication platform — async coordination channel per agency
- Dashboard / BI tool — shared performance visibility

## Steps

### Weekly Coordination Cadence
1. Fractional CMO sets recurring weekly sync (30 min max) with all active agencies
2. Each agency submits a status update 24h before the sync (template: what shipped, what's blocked, what's next)
3. Fractional CMO reviews updates, flags conflicts or overlaps before the call
4. During sync: review shared KPI dashboard, resolve blockers, confirm next-week priorities
5. Marketing Director publishes meeting notes within 4h to shared channel

### Onboarding a New Agency
1. Fractional CMO defines scope of work document with explicit boundaries (channels, budgets, deliverables, reporting cadence)
2. Marketing Director creates shared folder structure and grants platform access per Vendor Access SOP (see `07-vendor-access.md`)
3. Fractional CMO introduces new agency to existing agencies in a joint kickoff call — clarify who owns what
4. New agency receives: naming convention guide, UTM standard, brand guidelines, reporting template, escalation contacts
5. First 2 weeks: daily async check-in. Week 3+: standard weekly cadence

### Offboarding an Agency
1. Fractional CMO confirms offboarding decision with internal stakeholders
2. Marketing Director collects all deliverables, assets, and documentation from agency
3. Revoke all platform access per Vendor Access SOP (see `07-vendor-access.md`)
4. Fractional CMO conducts exit review: what worked, what didn't, lessons learned
5. Redistribute scope to remaining agencies or flag hiring need

### Scope Boundary Management
1. Each agency's scope is documented in a single shared matrix (channel x agency)
2. Any scope change requires written approval from Fractional CMO
3. If two agencies claim the same channel or audience segment, Fractional CMO arbitrates within 48h
4. Quarterly scope review: confirm boundaries still match business objectives

## Escalation
- If agency misses 2 consecutive weekly syncs without notice → Fractional CMO escalates to agency leadership
- If scope conflict is unresolved after 48h → Fractional CMO makes binding decision and documents rationale
- If agency consistently misses SLAs (3+ occurrences in 30 days) → trigger contract review

## KPIs

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Agency response time (async) | < 24h |
| Weekly sync attendance | 100% |
| Naming convention compliance | 100% |
| Budget accuracy (actual vs. approved) | +/- 5% |
| Scope conflicts (open) | Zero |

## Review Cadence
Monthly — Fractional CMO reviews agency performance, scope matrix, and escalation log. Quarterly deep review with internal leadership.
